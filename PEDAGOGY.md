# Math Facts Acquisition - Pedagogical Framework

**For:** K-3 Students (ages 5-8)  
**Subject:** Addition Facts (sums to 20)  
**Methodology:** Direct Instruction with Spiky POVs  
**Goal:** Accurate retrieval before speed training

---

## Our Spiky POVs (Pedagogical Stances)

**SPOV = Spiky Point of View** - A stance that disagrees with conventional educational consensus

### 1. Accuracy Before Speed âš¡

**Conventional View:** "Practice makes perfect" - drill facts until students get faster

**Our SPOV:** Accurate retrieval MUST be established before any speed work

**Implementation:**
- 85% mastery required on untimed quizzes before advancing
- No timed drills in acquisition phase
- Students take as long as they need per question
- Fluency training only AFTER acquisition complete

**Why This Works:** Practicing incorrect retrieval reinforces errors. Speed comes naturally once accuracy is automatic.

---

### 2. Series Saying Over Random Mixing ðŸ“Š

**Conventional View:** Mix facts randomly to prevent "pattern memorization"

**Our SPOV:** Teach facts in predictable sequences to reduce cognitive load

**Implementation:**
- Series Saying: 2+1, 3+1, 4+1, 5+1 (predictable pattern)
- Same first operand across a set
- Students hear/see the pattern building
- Makes initial retrieval easier

**Why This Works:** Reduces working memory demands during initial learning. Patterns help, they don't hurt.

---

### 3. Explicit Commutative Property Teaching ðŸ”„

**Conventional View:** "Students will discover that 2+3 = 3+2 through exploration"

**Our SPOV:** Explicitly teach turnarounds as a distinct concept

**Implementation:**
- Fact Families format teaches: "5+7 and 7+5 are turnarounds"
- Shows both facts side-by-side
- Audio: "They have the same sum. They're a fact family!"
- Practice asking "What's the turnaround of 5+7?"

**Why This Works:** Doubles fact knowledge with minimal effort. Explicit teaching is faster than discovery.

---

### 4. 85% Mastery Gating ðŸšª

**Conventional View:** "Move on when most students get it" (60-70% threshold)

**Our SPOV:** No student advances without demonstrated mastery

**Implementation:**
- 85% required on quiz to advance
- Quiz must be passed before next lesson unlocks
- No "moving on" to keep the class together
- Individual pacing respected

**Why This Works:** Prevents knowledge gaps from accumulating. Better to master 13 lessons than half-learn 26.

---

### 5. Immediate Error Correction ðŸ› ï¸

**Conventional View:** "Let students figure it out" (constructivist discovery)

**Our SPOV:** Provide immediate, explicit correction following DI protocols

**Implementation:**
- Student gets fact wrong â†’ Immediate correction with audio

- Test: "What is 4+1?" (immediate re-test)
- Re-test: Include corrected fact 2-3 questions later
- Never punitive, always supportive

**Why This Works:** Minimizes practice of errors. Students learn the RIGHT answer immediately.

## Teaching Formats

### Format 1: Series Saying (Odd Lessons)

**When:** Initial introduction of new facts  
**Structure:** 5-6 steps (some lessons add rule introduction)  
**Pattern:** Same first operand (2+1, 3+1, 4+1, 5+1)

**Steps:**
1. **Modeled Instruction** - Teacher reads, student listens
2. **(Optional Rule)** - For +1 and +0, explicit rule taught
3. **Read Together** - Choral reading with audio
4. **Type Sums** - Student types answers while hearing facts
5. **Recall in Order** - Guided recall without audio
6. **Recall from Memory** - Full recall, different order
7. **Quiz** - Different order, 85% to pass

### Format 2: Fact Families (Even Lessons)

**When:** After Series Saying (teaches turnarounds)  
**Structure:** 7 steps  
**Focus:** Commutative property (5+7 â†” 7+5)

**Steps:**
1. **Introduction** - Explain fact families concept (full on L2/L4, short on L6+)
2. **Review** - Show both facts: "1+2=3" and "2+1=3"
3. **Turnarounds** - Ask "What's the turnaround?" (verbal response)
4. **Practice** - Type turnaround with two-input system
5. **Quiz Intro** - "Quiz Time!" transition screen
6. **Turnaround Quiz** - Test turnaround knowledge
7. **Mixed Review** - 50% current + 50% previous turnarounds

## Instructional Principles

### 1. Minimize Cognitive Load
- Large text (text-6xl for equations)
- Number pad (familiar calculator interface)
- One task at a time
- Audio guidance throughout

### 2. Immediate Feedback
- Visual: âœ“ (green) or âœ— (red)
- Audio: Confirming or correcting
- Never leave student wondering if they're right

### 3. Distributed Practice
- Mixed reviews include previous lessons
- Weight towards harder facts (6,7,8,9)
- Spaced retrieval built into structure

### 4. Errorless Learning (Goal)
- "Virtually errorless" during teaching steps
- Errors corrected immediately
- High success rate builds confidence

### 5. Mastery-Based Progression
- Can't skip ahead
- Can't advance without 85%
- Respects individual pacing
- No pressure to keep up with class

---

## Assessment Philosophy

### Formative Assessment (During Lessons)
- Every step is a check for understanding
- Audio cues guide student
- Error correction happens in real-time
- No grades, just learning

### Summative Assessment (End of Lesson)
- Quiz tests all facts from lesson
- Different order than teaching
- 85% threshold enforced
- Can retry if failed (whole lesson resets)

### Comprehensive Assessment (End of Course)
- 26 questions (1 per lesson)
- Section 1: Regular sums (odd lessons)
- Section 2: Turnarounds (even lessons)
- Diagnostic: Shows which lessons need review
- Gateway to fluency phase

## Design for K-3 (Ages 5-8)

### Visual Design
- **Large text:** 6xl-9xl font sizes for equations
- **Bright colors:** Blue, green, orange gradients
- **Minimal text:** Audio-driven, not reading-heavy
- **Clear feedback:** Big âœ“ and âœ— symbols
- **Progress visible:** Progress bar, step indicators

### Interaction Design
- **Number pad:** Familiar, tap-friendly (not typing)
- **One action per screen:** Never overwhelming
- **Audio first:** Can complete without reading
- **Forgiving:** Clear button always available
- **Encouraging:** "Great job!", never "Wrong!"

### Cognitive Considerations
- **Working memory:** One fact at a time, predictable sequences
- **Attention span:** Steps last 2-5 minutes each
- **Success rate:** High (targeting 85%+ during teaching)
- **Autonomy:** Self-paced, can take breaks between lessons
- **Confidence:** Mastery builds, never feel "behind"
